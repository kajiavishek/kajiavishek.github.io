MY MOM

She tells me not to be an artist. I'm an engineer, I assure her. She carefully observes my designs, engineering drawings, and pictures from my research work. This looks like art! She jokes.

 

To some people, including my mom, I'm an engineer pretending to be an artist. To others, an artist pretending to be an engineer.

 

 

MY DESIGN

 

I want them to trigger questions, even doubts. Why does this look like it's floating in space? This structure isn't going to hold, is it? The less obvious the answers, the better the outcome. The larger the doubts, the more engagement my design demands from a viewer. To seek those answers. And in that process discover the intricacy and complexity of thought process, design approach, and engineering behind each piece.

 

 

MY EDUCATION

 

I attended Williams College in MA and Dartmouth College in NH. However, my real education comes from practicing minimalism; in design and lifestyle. It's not about settling for less. But deriving more from less things. All my works employ minimalist principles in one from or the other. From aesthetics to reducing material use to increasing prototyping efficiency.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

LIGHT

 

 

LIGHT

A 3D printed LED lamp inspired by the structure of Araucaria araucana plant. In addition to serving asethetic functions, the wooden sticks support an LED circuit inside the central dome. The design reduces printing time and allows easy installation of LEDs.

 

SPACE

 

 

SPACE

How do you make a transparent structure from opaque materials? How do you make a light-weight structure without compromising strength? Using grid structures of wooden panels and careful metal reinforcements in high stress areas, this studio chair fulfills both constraints. The chair was exbhited at Dartmouth's Hopkins Center Rotunda.

 

FLIGHT

 

FLIGHT

A drawing matured inside a sketchbook for a year finally took off to become a 3D model and eventually a sculpture. Made from 3D printed material and acrylic, this piece is an exploration into elegance of negative spaces as much as structural stability.

 

SKY

A dark room installation that takes you on a voyage to distant stars and galaxies. Suspended in space and hand-crafted, everyone who's viewed it has reacted with "what is this?" The question completes the piece and no answer is given.

 

GRAVITY

 

GRAVITY

Three separate objects made from and acrylic and 3D printed material compose a delicately balanced structure. The objects aren't glued or attached to each other. Thus, each contribute equally to the overall stability of the structure. Weight differentials of acrylic and 3D printed material also play an important role.
